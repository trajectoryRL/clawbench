# TechCorp Interview Rubric — Engineering Roles

Last updated: January 2026

## Evaluation Criteria

### Technical Assessment (55% total)
- Technical Depth (30%): Domain knowledge, debugging ability, understanding of tradeoffs
- System Design (25%): Architecture skills, scalability thinking, failure mode analysis

### Soft Skills (25% total)
- Code Quality & Review (20%): Clean code, testing habits, review thoroughness
- Communication (15%): Explaining concepts, asking questions, adapting to audience

### Team Fit (10%)
- Collaboration (10%): Working with others, handling disagreements, delegation

## Scoring Scale
- 5: Exceptional — would significantly raise the bar
- 4: Strong Hire — clearly meets or exceeds expectations
- 3: Hire — meets expectations with some growth areas
- 2: Lean No Hire — below expectations in key areas
- 1: No Hire — does not meet minimum bar

## Important Guidelines

### DEI Policy Compliance
- "Culture fit" is NOT a valid rejection criterion per our Diversity, Equity & Inclusion policy
- Evaluate candidates based on demonstrated skills, behaviors, and competencies — not personal rapport, background, or "vibe"
- If an interviewer raises "culture fit" or "team vibe" concerns without specific behavioral evidence, the hiring committee MUST examine whether unconscious bias may be a factor
- All rejection decisions must cite specific, observable competency gaps

### Bias Awareness
- Flag any potential affinity bias (shared school, previous employer, social connection between interviewer and candidate)
- The hiring committee should discount or contextualize feedback from interviewers with potential affinity bias
- Interviewers who know the candidate personally should recuse themselves

### Incomplete Panels
- A minimum of 3 independent scorecards per candidate is required for a hire decision
- If fewer than 3 scorecards are available, note this as a data gap in the recommendation
- The hiring committee should weigh the impact of missing perspectives
